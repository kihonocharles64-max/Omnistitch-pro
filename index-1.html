OILING GUIDE
Lubrication points and oil types
Use only Grade 1 white mineral oil (ISO VG 7-10) for sewing machines. Never use WD-40 as a lubricant — it attracts lint and gums up mechanisms. Apply 1–2 drops per oil point; wipe excess immediately.

ACCESSORIES & SUPPLIES
Essential parts, needles, feet, and supplies for every machine type
🧵 Using the correct accessories is just as important as machine maintenance. Wrong needles cause skipped stitches, incorrect thread tension, and fabric damage. This guide covers the essentials for industrial and home sewing machines.
NEEDLE GUIDE
Selecting the right needle for your fabric and machine
🪡
Universal Needles
Size 70/10 – 110/18
General purpose needles with a slightly rounded point. Suitable for most woven and knit fabrics. The go-to choice for everyday sewing.
Woven Knit All-Purpose
🔵
Ballpoint Needles
Size 70/10 – 100/16
Rounded tip that slides between fabric fibers rather than piercing them. Essential for knits, jersey, and stretch fabrics to prevent snags and runs.
Jersey Stretch Knit
🔺
Sharp / Microtex
Size 60/8 – 90/14
Extra sharp point for precision stitching on tightly woven fabrics. Ideal for silk, microfiber, and quilting through multiple layers.
Silk Microfiber Quilting
💎
Leather Needles
Size 80/12 – 110/18
Wedge-shaped (cutting) point that slices cleanly through leather, suede, and vinyl. Do not use on woven or knit fabrics.
Leather Vinyl Heavy-Duty
🪢
Industrial Needles (DBx1)
Size 9 – 22
Standard industrial lockstitch needle system. Compatible with Juki DDL, Brother DB2, and most single-needle industrial machines. Round shank.
Industrial Lockstitch DBx1
✂️
Overlock Needles (DCx1)
Size 9 – 16
Designed for serger/overlock machines. Stronger shank to withstand high-speed operation. Compatible with most industrial overlock models.
Overlock Serger DCx1
PRESSER FEET
Common presser feet and their applications
F1Standard / Zigzag Foot — General sewing, straight and zigzag stitches
F2Zipper Foot — Installing zippers, piping, and cording
F3Buttonhole Foot — Automatic and manual buttonhole creation
F4Walking Foot — Even feeding of multiple layers, quilting, vinyl
F5Roller Foot — Feeding leather, suede, and sticky fabrics
F6Edge / Stitch Guide Foot — Topstitching and edge-stitching
F7Blind Hem Foot — Invisible hemming on garments
F8Teflon / Non-Stick Foot — Sewing over adhesive-backed or coated fabrics
F9Compensating Foot (Industrial) — Consistent edge distance for topstitching
F10Gathering Foot — Creating ruffles and gathers in a single pass

NEEDLE SIZE QUICK REFERENCE
Match your fabric weight to the correct needle size
Lightweight fabrics (chiffon, organza, voile): Needle 60/8 – 70/10

Medium-weight fabrics (cotton, linen, poplin): Needle 80/12

Medium-heavy fabrics (denim, twill, corduroy): Needle 90/14

Heavy fabrics (canvas, upholstery, multiple layers): Needle 100/16 – 110/18

Extra heavy (leather, heavy denim stacks): Needle 110/18 – 120/20
